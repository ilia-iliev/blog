Top 10 Linux Commands You Should Know
2025-09-15

Boost your productivity with these essential terminal commands. Whether you're a developer, sysadmin, or just curious, the command line is one of the most powerful tools at your disposal.

Start with the basics: ls, cd, pwd, and cat. These four commands let you navigate the filesystem and read files. Master them and you'll never feel lost in a terminal again.

Next level: grep, find, and awk. These are your search and transformation tools. Need to find a string across thousands of files? grep. Need to locate a file by name? find. Need to extract a column from a CSV? awk.

Power tools: ssh, rsync, and tmux. Remote into servers, sync files efficiently, and manage multiple terminal sessions. These three commands alone can replace a dozen GUI applications.
